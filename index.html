Lopar - the perfect tourist destination

The town of Lopar (Rab, Croatia) is a perfect vacation destination thanks to really pleasant Mediterranean climate and preserved nature. Lopar is a perfect location for a relaxing holiday, but also offers a lot of attractions for adrenaline seeking visitors. This tourist venue has a lot of beautiful sandy beaches and offers a variety of sports and leisure activities. First tourists visited this town more than 100 years ago and, since that time, it has continued to develop as a tourist destination. Visit Lopar and experience the perfect holiday.

Sports and Entertainment in Lopar

After finding and settling in to suitable accommodation, Lopar will surprise you with a variety of sports and leisure activities which you can enjoy during your vacation. If you are the athletic type or if you just want to see and experience something new, you can spend an active holiday in Lopar. These are the sports and entertainment facilities our town offers (and almost every summer something new appears): Night club, Aquapark, Kayak, Diving, Water Ball, Aquagun, Boat trips, Jet ski, Parasailing, Tennis, Miniature golf, Running tracks, Trampoline, Football, Basketball, Table Tennis, Hiking, Bungee trampoline, Wind surfing, Beach volleyball, Cocktail bars and excursions to the Islands of Goli and Grgur.

Landmarks and History of Lopar

On the territory of Lopar, there are traces of settlement that go back in history all the way to the New Stone Age. This settlement lasted through the Stone Age, Copper and Bronze Age until 4th century AD when, on the same location, an ancient Greek military fort was built. When it comes to inhabitants, St. Marin is the most important historical figure of Lopar and the whole island of Rab. He was a stonemason and is known for founding the first republic in the world called San Marino. Saint Marin's image is located on the emblem of the town of Lopar.

Island of Rab - places and information

If you wish to travel to Lopar, you should know some facts about the whole island of Rab. It is located in Primorsko Goranska County, in the Kvarner bay, on the North side of the Adriatic Sea, right next to island Krk and Cres. It is 22km long and it has 8 settlements: town Rab, Lopar, Banjol, Barbat, Kampor, Mundanije, Palit and Supetarska Draga. According to the census of 2011, the island of Rab has less than 9000 inhabitants. Among historical figures, the most noteworthy is Ivan Krstitelj Rabljanin (c. 1470. 1540.) who was a bronze caster, technologist and artist. Although Rab is one of the smaller islands on the Adriatic, it is extremely popular for its tourist attractions, sights, landmarks and preserved nature.
